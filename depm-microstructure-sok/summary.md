# SoK: Market Microstructure for Decentralized Prediction Markets (DePMs)

**Authors:** Jeremy Clark et al.
**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.15612
**Published:** October 2025 (v3: March 2026)
**Recommendation:** YES (comprehensive reference)

## Summary

A systematization of knowledge paper that reviews the history of DePMs (dating back to 2011, including Truthcoin and Augur v1). Presents a **modular workflow comprising eight stages**: underlying infrastructure, market topic, share structure and pricing, market initialization, trading, market resolution, settlement, and archiving.

### Key Contribution

For each module, enumerates design variants and analyzes trade-offs around:
- Decentralization
- Expressiveness
- Manipulation resistance

### Why Modern DePMs Differ

Polymarket deviates materially from earlier designs like Truthcoin and Augur v1 — the paper explains how and why.

### Relevance

Foundational reference for understanding prediction market design space. Useful for evaluating which market features create arbitrage opportunities and where microstructure vulnerabilities lie.

### Key Takeaway

The modular framework provides a structured way to think about prediction market design. Each module's design choices affect arbitrage opportunities, manipulation vectors, and information efficiency.
