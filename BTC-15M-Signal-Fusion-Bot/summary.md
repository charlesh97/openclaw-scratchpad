# Summary: Arbitrage Analysis in Polymarket NBA Markets

**arXiv:2605.00864** | Apr 2026

## One-Page TL;DR

Systematic empirical analysis of algorithmic arbitrage within Polymarket's NBA game markets using **75M+ limit order book snapshots** across **173 games**.

### Key Findings
1. **Single-market anomalies** — extremely rare (only 7 episodes), median duration **3.6 seconds**
2. **Combinatorial inefficiencies** — more frequent (290 episodes), concentrated in final minutes of games
3. **Median combinatorial return** — **101 basis points** (statistically meaningful)
4. **Theoretical "Middle" jackpot** — never empirically realized
5. **Liquidity bottleneck** — 76.9% of opportunities constrained to **14.8 shares average**

### Bottom Line
"While executable mispricings exist, they are structurally bounded by liquidity, confining risk-free extraction strictly to the retail scale."

### Why This Matters for Us
- Confirms that sports markets are less efficient than crypto markets for arb
- The liquidity bottleneck is the key constraint — our detection must account for depth
- 101bps median return is attractive even at retail scale
