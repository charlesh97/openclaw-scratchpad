# Toward Black-Scholes for Prediction Markets

**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.15205  
**Authors:** Pre-print  
**Published:** October 2025 (updated April 2026)  
**Type:** Academic paper (theoretical finance)

## Summary

Proposes a **logit jump-diffusion with risk-neutral drift** as a foundational pricing model for prediction markets — the equivalent of what Black-Scholes did for options. Treats the traded probability p_t as a Q-martingale and exposes belief volatility, jump intensity, and dependence as quotable risk factors.

### Key Contributions
- Unified pricing kernel for prediction markets
- Risk-neutral martingale probability framework
- Jump-diffusion for sudden probability shifts (e.g., news events)
- Opens the door to prediction market derivatives

## Why It Matters

- **First unified pricing model** for prediction markets — currently there's no standard
- Could enable option-like strategies on prediction markets (e.g., volatility trading)
- Risk factor quoting would allow hedging of prediction market exposure
- Theoretical foundation for more sophisticated products

## Implementability: 1/5

- Highly theoretical — requires significant financial engineering to implement
- Unclear if Polymarket or Kalshi will adopt such models
- Valuable as conceptual framework for strategy development
- Worth monitoring for practical implementations
