# Designing Automated Market Makers for Combinatorial Securities

**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.08972  
**Recommendation:** NO  
**Published:** November 2024  

## What It Does

Academic paper on designing automated market makers (AMMs) for combinatorial securities. Introduces the combinatorial swap operation problem and shows it can be reduced to range update problems. Relevant for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of prediction market AMM design.

## Why It Matters (Mildly)

- Theoretical foundation for combinatorial market making
- Shows AMM design trade-offs between computational efficiency and arbitrage
- Useful context for understanding Polymarket's market maker mechanics

## Risks

- Purely theoretical — no implementation or empirical results
- 2024 paper, pre-Polymarket's CLOB migration
- Not directly applicable to Polymarket's current order-book model

## Implementability: 1/5

Purely academic. No practical implementation path.
